A Bit About Me

Hey there! I'm Matt, and I hail from the faraway land of Waynesboro, VA. I grew up running, swimming, and playing tennis there, and then came to the University of Virginia three years ago. At UVA, I hoped to find new interests both in and outside of school-- and that I did. I joined the Club Frisbee team and Intervarsity Christian Fellowship, and those became my home away from home. I have also become more involved in volunteering opportunities throughout my time at UVA. I started tutoring through Madison House my first year, and have since become involved in Madison House leadership. I also became a brother of Alpha Phi Omega, the co-ed service fraternity on ground that has changed my worldview. By far the most incredible service opportunity that I've had, though, has been biking across the country for affordable housing.

I was always interested in computer science, but I was looking for an interesting application of CS. I found that in biomedical engineering, where I declared my major and joined the Computational Systems Biology lab under the direction of Dr. Jason Papin. I also dove further into the field with a summer internship at the National Institutes of Health in Bethesda, MD. Over time, however, I began to realize that my passions lay in Computer Science, so I declared a double major in the CS department and began to heavily pursue CS. I found an internship on the Health and Sciences team at Palantir Technologies, and I will be joining the company for a full-time position next fall. For now, I'm just trying to enjoy every day that I have left here and to take in everything that I can!
